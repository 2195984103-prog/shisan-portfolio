# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

This portfolio is primarily for recruiters evaluating Shisan as a visual designer. They arrive with limited time and need to quickly judge taste level, commercial execution ability, craft, and whether the work feels mature enough for real client or in-house brand contexts.

## Product Purpose

The site presents selected visual design, brand content, and AIGC workflow projects in a way that feels polished, credible, and commercially grounded. Success means a recruiter immediately reads the work as refined, sees evidence of production judgment, and can move from overview to project details without friction.

## Brand Personality

Refined, commercial, mature.

The experience should feel like a carefully edited design portfolio rather than a resume page. It should carry the calm confidence of premium brand material: generous spacing, precise alignment, strong imagery, restrained copy, and clear project hierarchy.

## Anti-references

Do not make the site feel like a flashy technology landing page, a heavy sci-fi interface, an overly skeuomorphic portfolio, a social-media template, or a literal resume layout. Avoid excessive roundness, decorative gradients, loud UI effects, generic card grids, and overused uppercase labels on every section.

## Design Principles

1. Let the work feel expensive.
   Project images and case-study surfaces should carry the page. UI chrome should support the imagery, not compete with it.

2. Make judgment visible.
   Layout, spacing, type scale, and image crops should show mature visual control. Every detail should look deliberately placed.

3. Keep the reading path calm.
   Recruiters should understand role, project type, brand, and visual strength quickly, without dense resume-style scanning.

4. Use restraint as confidence.
   Prefer quiet hierarchy, strong whitespace, small-radius refinement, and precise transitions over decorative effects.

5. Show commercial range without visual noise.
   Automotive, beauty, offline material, PPT, and AIGC projects can vary in content, but the surrounding portfolio system should remain cohesive.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA contrast for text and interactive elements. Preserve keyboard focus states, readable Chinese and English typography, reduced-motion fallbacks, and responsive layouts that do not overlap or crop important text on mobile.
